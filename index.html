Zijian Guo

About me

I am a Qiushi Chair Professor in the Data Science Center at Zhejiang University. I received my B.S. in Mathematics from The Chinese University of Hong Kong in 2012 and my Ph.D. in Statistics from the University of Pennsylvania in 2017, where I was fortunate to be advised by Professor T. Tony Cai(COPSS Award laureate). From 2017 to 2025, I served on the faculty of the Department of Statistics at Rutgers University, advancing from Assistant Professor to tenured Associate Professor, before returning to China to join Zhejiang University in 2025.

[Google Scholar] [arXiv] [Github]

Research

I am broadly interested in causal inference, high-dimensional statistics, multi-source and transfer learning, distributionally robust optimization, non-regular statistical inference, and statistical methods at the interface of optimization and statistics. A substantial part of my work focuses on robust learning and inference under heterogeneity and distribution shift, with applications in health and genetics.
My work has appeared in the Annals of Statistics, Journal of the Royal Statistical Society: Series B, Cell Genomics, Journal of the American Statistical Association, Biometrika, Journal of Machine Learning Research, and the Journal of Econometrics. I currently serve on the editorial boards of the Journal of the American Statistical Association (Theory and Methods) and TEST.
